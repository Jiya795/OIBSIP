About This Project
My submission for Task 2 of Level 2 in the Web Development track — a tribute
page for Kalpana Chawla, the first woman of Indian origin to fly to space.
I picked her because her story felt like it had real weight to it: someone
from a small town who had to push past people telling her aerospace wasn't
a field for women, and a mission that ended in tragedy that's still
remembered today.

Tech Stack
- HTML5
- CSS3 (no JavaScript needed for this one)

Features
- Hero section with a circular portrait frame, her name, life dates, and a
  short intro line, styled in an elegant blue-and-white memorial theme
  (inspired by classic tribute/memorial layouts)
- About section with four original paragraphs covering her early life,
  education, first spaceflight, and the final mission — written from my own
  research, not copied from any single source
- A chronological timeline from her birth in 1962 through to STS-107 in
  2003, laid out with year markers down the left and details on the right
- A quote block for something she said while in orbit during STS-87,
  set against a deep navy panel for contrast
- Two distinct background tones used across sections (soft powder-blue
  and white panels, with a deep navy accent for the stat strip and quote)
- Two typefaces — Cormorant Garamond for display headings/quotes, EB Garamond
  for body text — both classic serif faces to match the formal, elegant tone
- Fully responsive layout (the timeline and stat strip both stack cleanly on
  mobile widths)

About the Photo
I left a labeled placeholder box in the hero section instead of hotlinking
